Per name: (1) key questions & what FactSet data answers, (2) residual trader-judgment questions, (3) **lockup schedule**, (4) **comps** — FY26→FY27E consensus growth, estimate-revision breadth (the cleanest "in/out of rotation" measure: # of analysts revising up vs. down last cycle), multiple context, and cycle history.

## 1. SpaceX — SPCX ($152, +9.9%)
**Data answered:** absorption (35 analysts; Nasdaq-100 add 7/7), monetization (FY26E rev ~$39.4B → FY27E ~$74.5B, +89%; Starlink $11.4B FY25 revenue, 10.3M subs +105% y/y, the only profitable segment), currency validity (all-stock Cursor deal, $60B implied, Q3 close).
**Trader residual:** PT range $131–$800 (σ≈48% of mean) — orbital datacenter/AI optionality is unpriceable; equity (+10%) vs. IG bonds (selling off late June) divergence; float mechanics below.

**Lockup schedule (the most complex on the list; float at IPO only ~4–5% of 13.08B shares):**
- 20% of eligible shares: 2nd trading day after Q2'26 earnings (projected ~Aug 6)
- +10% performance trigger: if SPCX ≥$175.50 (130% of IPO) on 5 of 10 consecutive days pre-earnings
- Time tranches ~7% each at 70/90/105/120/135 days (≈ Aug 21 / Sep 10 / Sep 25 / Oct 10 / Oct 25)
- Up to ~28% more after Q3 earnings (Oct–Dec window)
- Full 180-day block expires **Dec 8, 2026**
- **Musk (~6.4B sh) + significant investors: 366-day lock, no early release — nothing until Jun 12, 2027.** Combined extended block ~7.8B sh (>60% of pre-IPO). 2026 = drumbeat of partial unlocks into a tiny float; 2027 = the real supply event.

**Comps & rotation (no true comp; space + AI-infra basket):**
- RKLB (FY27E growth +39%; revisions **12 up / 1 down — in rotation**), ASTS (+381% off tiny base but **1 up / 7 down — being cut, out of rotation**), IRDM (+2%; static, value/yield telecom)
- Cycle: space cohort = 2021 SPAC boom → 2022–23 bust (RKLB to ~$3) → 2024–25 defense/constellation rally → per StreetAccount 6/5/26, space pure-plays saw double-digit declines in the June risk-off. SPCX at ~50x FY26E / ~27x FY27E sales trades far above RKLB (~10–15x~) — the premium is Starlink cash flow + optionality, which is why the comp set barely disciplines the valuation. That's the trader debate in a nutshell.

## 2. Cerebras — CBRS (~$198 vs $185 offer; $386 intraday high)
**Data answered:** street conviction intact — 10 of 12 positive, PT mean $291 (+47%); FY26E $862M → FY27E $2.95B (**+242%**, 8 of 10 revised up); OpenAI 750MW modeled as bookable; G42 now 24% of revenue (vs 85% in '24).
**Trader residual:** the multiple on concentrated contract revenue (~70x FY26E → ~20x FY27E sales at current price); AI-capex-digestion beta.

**Lockup schedule (staged, no single cliff):**
- Non-exec employees: 7.5% sellable day one + 7.5% day two (33%-above-IPO trigger hit on debut)
- Directors/officers/pre-IPO investors: 15% after Q1'26 earnings report
- Further releases after Q2 earnings + fixed dates in Aug, Sep, Oct 2026
- Final tranche: earlier of post-Q3-earnings release or the 180-day outside date ≈ **Nov 9, 2026**
- Note: early supply already flowing — part of why the debut spike faded.

**Comps & rotation (AI compute):**
- NVDA (FY27E +43%; **50 up / 1 down**), AMD (+55%; **42 up / 1 down**), CRWV (+97%; 11 up / 5 down — mixed)
- Cycle: the 2026 story is *broadening* — by mid-May, Intel/AMD/Micron were each +80%+ in a month ("silicon renaissance," bets spreading beyond NVDA), which is exactly the window CBRS priced into. Then the June 5 risk-off hit "Memory, AI Enablers incl. chipmakers." Rotation verdict: **core AI semis still firmly in rotation by revision breadth; the speculative fringe is churning.** CBRS at ~20x FY27E sales vs NVDA/AMD at ~10–15x~ forward means it must out-grow the group forever or converge — the comp math is the bear case; the revision momentum is the bull case.

## 3. INNIO — INIO ($33.7, +24%)
**Data answered:** durable cycle (FY26E $3.79B → FY27E $5.13B, +35%; PT $44.6 = +32%); sponsor-exit stigma absent. **First catalyst: confirmed Q2 earnings 7/28, BMO + call.**
**Trader residual:** flow — Advent still ~88%; follow-on cadence unmodelable.

**Lockup schedule:**

- Practical overhang is bigger than the date: Advent's 660M retained shares will come via marketed follow-ons for years; every strong print is a potential deal window. Greenshoe (13.5M) already absorbed.

**Comps & rotation (power equipment / engines):**
- GEV (FY27E +14%; revisions **0 up / 2 down — momentum stalling** after its monster 2024–25 run), CMI (+8%; **14 up / 1 down — in rotation**), GNRC (+14%; quiet), CAT (+11%; 6–7 up)
- Cycle: power equipment was *the* 2024–25 industrial trade (GEV ~5x off its spin). 2026: revision leadership has migrated from GEV (plateauing) to engine makers (CMI, and INNIO's own +35% growth screens best-in-class). INIO at ~6.7x FY26E / ~4.9x FY27E sales sits between GNRC (~2x~) and GEV (~2.5–3x~ but slower growth) — on growth-adjusted terms INIO screens cheap vs the group, which is what the +24% move and $44.6 PTs are arbitraging. Rotation verdict: **gas-engine niche in rotation; turbine mega-caps digesting.**

## 4. Madison Air — MAIR ($35.8, +27%)
**Data answered:** street unanimity — PT $48.1 in a $45–50 band; **11 of 12 revised up**; but consensus growth only +6.6% (FY26E $3.84B → FY27E $4.09B) — the rally is margin/multiple, not top line.
**Trader residual:** is IAQ a premium category or HVAC in a costume — pure multiple opinion, made sharper by the crowd being on one side.

**Lockup schedule:**
- Standard 180-day from 4/15 pricing → expires ≈ **Oct 12, 2026** — **the earliest major lockup expiry on this screen.**
- **Cornerstones were never locked:** Counterpoint Global, Durable Capital, HRTG (~$525M indicated) took stock with no lockup — supply from them is possible any day and unknowable.
- Greenshoe (12.4M) fully exercised. Madison Industries retains control (Class B).

**Comps & rotation (HVAC / building systems):**
- TT (FY27E +8.6%), LII (+5.2%), CARR (+4.9%) — revisions modestly positive (1–5 up each), **sector steady-in-rotation, not hot**
- Cycle: TT/LII re-rated massively 2023–24 on data-center cooling + margin execution to premium multiples (~25–30x~ fwd P/E) while CARR lagged (~18–20x~). MAIR's +27% since April with only 6–7% growth implies the market is slotting it into the TT/LII premium bucket immediately. The comp question traders must answer: does an IAQ pure-play deserve the *top* of the HVAC multiple stack out of the gate, in a sector whose own revision momentum is mild? The Oct 12 unlock + free-floating cornerstones are the test dates.

## 5. Fervo — FRVO ($27.35, ~flat)
**Data answered:** capital available (upsized, above range); street believes (PT $46 = +70%); consensus itself confirms the equity is pre-revenue: FY26E $6.4M, FY27E $79M — everything is Cape Station (first power target: end-2026).
**Trader residual:** probability × discount rate on first-of-kind drilling execution. No dataset exists.

**Lockup schedule:**
- Standard 180-day from 5/12 pricing → expires ≈ **Nov 8, 2026** (confirm early-release triggers in 424B4)
- Structural note: expiry lands *before* the first-power milestone — insiders get a sell window ahead of the binary event. Watch Form 4s in November; heavy insider selling pre-milestone would itself be information.

**Comps & rotation (clean-firm power):**
- ORA (FY27E +0.8%; 5 up / 0 down FY26 — steady utility, ~20x~ P/E, the "what geothermal is worth when it's boring" anchor), OKLO (FY27E median revenue **$1M** — pure optionality vehicle), BE (+73% FY27E growth; 6 up / 1 down — fuel cells re-rated on data-center demand)
- Cycle: clean-firm went from niche (2022–23) to mania (2024–25 nuclear/SMR parabolic, OKLO the poster child) to selective (June 2026 risk-off hit "Energy Storage" pure-plays double digits). FRVO at >125x FY27E sales is priced like OKLO-style optionality but with a nearer, drillable milestone — the trader question is whether it migrates toward ORA's boring multiple *with* ORA-scale megawatts (bull case: growth at utility risk) or stays in the optionality bucket that June showed gets sold first. Rotation verdict: **clean-firm optionality currently out of rotation; cash-flowing power in.**

## 6. Blackstone Digital Infrastructure Trust — BXDC ($21, +2.8%)
**Data answered:** yield-product treatment confirmed (6 of 12 at Hold; PT $23.8 = +13%); deployment pace is the modeled uncertainty (FY26E rev $7.8M–$99.6M — a 12x analyst spread — converging to ~$296M FY27E).
**Trader residual:** terminal cap rate for hyperscaler-leased shells; whether AI-tenant credit trades IG through a capex pause. Sets financing cost for the ~$1T pipeline.

**Lockup schedule:**
- Standard 180-day from 5/13 pricing → expires ≈ **Nov 9, 2026**
- Lower-drama than peers: external Blackstone manager (alignment via fees/retained interest rather than a big sellable insider block); greenshoe (13.1M) fully exercised. Real supply risk is future *primary* issuance to fund acquisitions — REITs grow by selling stock, so every pipeline announcement is potential dilution, lockup or not.

**Comps & rotation (data-center real estate):**
- EQIX (FY27E +10%), DLR (+11.5%), IRM (+8.7%) — revisions mildly positive (1–4 up), **sector in slow re-accumulation, not momentum rotation**
- Cycle: DLR/EQIX rallied with AI 2023–24, then EQIX's 2025 capex-guidance shock (~-15% in a day~) taught the market that hyperscaler demand can be a *cost* story for landlords; 2026 has been stabilization, with the group trading as rate-sensitive yield (~20–25x~ AFFO). BXDC is the pure-play version of the bull case (no legacy interconnection business, just leased shells) — it should trade tighter than DLR/EQIX if hyperscaler credit holds, wider if it cracks. Rotation verdict: **data-center REITs in the "own for income, not for beta" phase.**

## 7. DPC / Doncasters — DPC ($48.3, +44%)
**Data answered:** almost nothing — FactSet shows **zero ratings, targets, or estimates** (quiet period; priced 6/24). The +44% happened with no sell-side framework: pure buy-side price discovery on casting-capacity scarcity, ~28M-share float, QIA anchor.
**Trader residual:** everything, definitionally — until initiations land (imminent, ~late July) and the first print.

**Lockup schedule:**

- Concurrent private-placement buyers (existing holders ~$69M; **QIA $75M**) signed customary lockups per the prospectus — the anchor money is tied up into late December too
- With a float this small, the December unlock is proportionally one of the largest supply events on the list. +44% into a locked register is exactly the setup where expiry matters most.

**Comps & rotation (aero castings / superalloys):**
- HWM (FY27E +13.2%; revisions **20 up / 0 down — the single strongest breadth of all 28 comps pulled**), CRS (+10.7%; 2 up), ATI (+9.3%; flat)
- Cycle: this is the decade's quiet supercycle — HWM ~10x+ off 2020 lows, CRS ~8x off 2022 — driven by engine-aftermarket scarcity (LEAP/GTF shop visits) now compounded by IGT/data-center turbine demand. Perfect revision breadth at HWM says the trade is **fully in rotation with no fatigue in the data**. DPC at ~$7.0B market cap on ~$837M FY25 revenue (~8x+ trailing sales, well above HWM ~5–6x~) is being priced as the highest-torque, smallest-float way to own the bottleneck. The comp set justifies enthusiasm; the *premium to the comp set* is the trader opinion.

## 8. ERock — EROC ($13.8, -39%)
**Data answered:** buy-side rejected it pre-coverage (broke to ~$15.5 in the quiet period); the syndicate then initiated bullish into the wreck on 7/6 (JPM OW $24, Evercore OP $28, Wolfe OP $24, Barclays OW $23, Guggenheim Buy $23; BofA Neutral $16) — PT mean $22.6 = **+64%**, the widest street-vs-tape gap on the screen. Consensus models the backlog converting: FY26E $448M → FY27E $1.43B (+219%).
**Trader residual:** does $1.3B of backlog become delivered, margined megawatts on schedule, or does the structural discount (Up-C, insider proceeds, CA/TX concentration, losses) stay pinned on the multiple? First print (~Aug) referees.

**Lockup schedule:**
- Standard 180-day from 6/9 pricing → expires ≈ **Dec 6, 2026** (confirm early-release provisions)
- Up-C wrinkle: pre-IPO owners hold ~171M Class B units (~87%+ of economics) exchangeable into Class A — post-lockup, exchanges + registered secondaries are the supply path. A stock already -39% heading into a December unlock of a 10%-float Up-C is a setup traders will have strong opinions on in both directions (capitulation flush vs. too-late-to-sell).

**Comps & rotation (distributed / onsite power):**
- GNRC (FY27E +14%; quiet revisions), BE (+73%; 6 up / 1 down — **in rotation**), CAT (+11%; steady)
- Cycle: distributed power's history is boom-bust — GNRC's COVID boom/bust (-70% in 2022) is the cautionary template; BE's 2025–26 re-rating on data-center fuel cells is the current winner. EROC now trades ~2.1x FY27E sales vs BE at a premium multiple with comparable modeled growth — on paper the cheapest growth in the power complex, which is exactly what six initiating banks are pointing at. The market is saying paper growth ≠ delivered growth. Rotation verdict: **the theme is in rotation; EROC specifically has been ejected from it — re-entry requires a print, not a note.**

MNTN (May 2025) reopened the IPO window; LFTO priced above range confirming it. The comp data frames LFTO's question precisely: at ~16% growth it sits between APP (+31%, premium multiple ~8–10x~ sales) and TTD (+10%, de-rated ~4–5x~). Which gravity well captures it is unanswerable by estimates that already agree — it's a rotation bet on whether "AI performance marketing" reads as APP-like or TTD-like at the first print.

## 10. GMR Solutions — GMRS ($15.7, +3.9%)
**Data answered:** the workout cleared (deal + $500M placement done, 7 of 8 analysts positive, stock holds the cut price); growth agreed and flat (FY26E $6.01B → FY27E $6.36B, +5.7%); PT distribution exposes the fight — mean $19.4, median $17.5, **high $35 / low $14**.
**Trader residual:** deleveraging torque vs. reimbursement/labor erosion — same revenue line, 2.5x valuation range. Pure leverage-path opinion.

**Lockup schedule:**
- Standard 180-day from 5/12 pricing → expires ≈ **Nov 8, 2026**
- **The bigger supply story isn't the lockup:** KKR/Ares/HPS pledged ~14.8M shares + ~135.8M warrants (≈60.9% of post-IPO shares) as collateral for ~$500M of margin loans (JPMorgan Chase Bank, BofA) funding their private-placement purchase. Pledged collateral can move on lender action regardless of lockup sentiment — a mechanical overhang unique on this screen. Any sharp drawdown has a reflexive component.

**Comps & rotation (levered healthcare services):**
- BTSG (FY27E +13.5%; 2–3 up — the proof-of-concept: Jan-2024 KKR-backed IPO that worked), OPCH (+7.1%; slight down revisions), SGRY (+5.2%; **0 up / 1–2 down — drifting out**)
- Cycle: healthcare services spent 2023–24 in the labor-cost/reimbursement penalty box and never joined the AI-era rotation; the bid is value/deleveraging-specific, not sectoral (BTSG re-rated on execution; SGRY languished when its take-private hopes faded). GMRS at a discount multiple with 5–6% growth is priced like SGRY; the $35-target analyst is underwriting a BTSG path. Rotation verdict: **sector out of rotation; single-name deleveraging stories can still work — which is precisely the bet.**
