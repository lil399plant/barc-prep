## 2. Wimbledon — just ended Sunday (June 29–July 12)
- **Men's: Jannik Sinner defended the title**, beating Alexander Zverev 6-7, 7-6, 6-3, 6-4. Fifth slam, 100th major match win, **ten straight wins over Zverev**, and he's 38-3 on the year with a 5,000-point ranking lead — full-blown era territory. Zverev became the first man born in the 90s to reach all four major finals (won his first slam at this year's French).
- **Women's: Linda Nosková (21) won her first slam** in an all-Czech final over Karolína Muchová 6-2, 5-7, 6-3 — youngest Wimbledon champ since Kvitová in 2011, and the **tenth different women's champion in ten years** (the parity stat everyone quotes).
- Storylines with legs: **Carlos Alcaraz missed it entirely** (wrist injury, out two straight slams — the sport misses him badly); **Djokovic broke Federer's all-time Wimbledon match-wins record (107)** and played a 5h15m quarterfinal at his age; **Serena Williams came back** as a singles wildcard four years after retiring and lost R1; defending champ Świątek got bounced in R3 by Alexandra Eala (first Filipina to go that deep at a major); British wildcard Arthur Fery made a fairytale run to the semis.
- Prize money: £64.2M total pot, +20% y/y — the biggest jump in tournament history. Singles champs took ~$4.8M each.
